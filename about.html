About Dinagat Islands

A hidden paradise in the Philippines

Exploring the Hidden Paradise of Mindanao

Dinagat Islands is a beautiful island province located in the northeastern part of Mindanao in the Philippines. Known for its pristine white-sand beaches, crystal-clear turquoise waters, dramatic limestone rock formations, and untouched island landscapes, Dinagat Islands is often described as one of the country's hidden paradises. The province offers a peaceful and natural environment that attracts travelers who are looking for adventure, relaxation, and breathtaking scenery away from crowded tourist destinations.

The province is composed of several municipalities including San Jose, Basilisa, Libjo, Loreto, Cagdianao, Tubajon, and Dinagat. Each municipality offers its own unique attractions, from hidden beaches and island hopping destinations to historical landmarks and cultural experiences. San Jose, the provincial capital, serves as the main gateway to the islands and provides access to many tourism services and facilities.

With its growing tourism industry and preserved natural beauty, Dinagat Islands continues to attract visitors who want to explore one of Mindanao’s most remarkable destinations. Whether you are seeking adventure, scenic landscapes, or a quiet tropical escape, Dinagat Islands offers an unforgettable travel experience

History of Dinagat Islands

The Province of Dinagat Islands officially became an independent province on December 2, 2006, after separating from the province of Surigao del Norte through Republic Act No. 9355. Before gaining provincial status, the islands were originally part of Surigao del Norte and were administered as several municipalities under that province. The creation of Dinagat Islands as a separate province aimed to improve local governance, economic development, and public services for the island communities.

Long before modern political boundaries were established, the islands were already inhabited by indigenous groups and early settlers who relied on fishing, farming, and trading for their livelihood. The rich marine resources and fertile lands allowed communities to thrive while maintaining a close relationship with nature. Over time, migrants from nearby provinces in the Visayas and Mindanao also settled in the area, contributing to the province’s diverse culture and traditions.

Today, Dinagat Islands is recognized for its natural beauty and emerging eco-tourism industry, while still preserving its heritage and traditions.

Culture & Traditions

The culture of the Dinagat Islands is a blend of indigenous traditions and influences from settlers from nearby parts of Mindanao and the Visayas. These influences shaped the islands’ unique identity, where communities value strong family ties, cooperation, and respect for nature. Many residents still practice traditional livelihoods such as fishing and farming, which remain an important part of everyday life.

Festivals, music, dance, and local crafts highlight the creativity and vibrant spirit of the island communities. One of the most celebrated events is the Bugkosan Festival, held every December to commemorate the province’s founding anniversary. The festival features colorful street dances, cultural performances, and community celebrations that showcase local pride and traditions while bringing residents and visitors together.

Economy & Livelihood

The economy of the Dinagat Islands is mainly supported by fishing, farming, and small local businesses. Many residents rely on fishing as their primary source of income, while others grow crops such as coconut, rice, and vegetables. Handicraft making is also practiced in some communities, reflecting the creativity and traditions of the local people.

Tourism is also growing in the province as more visitors discover the natural beauty of Dinagat Islands. With its beaches, islands, and scenic landscapes, tourism is creating new opportunities for local jobs, small businesses, and sustainable development while encouraging the protection of the environment.
